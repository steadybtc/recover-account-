Recover Your Hacked or Disabled Account

Immediate steps you can take to get your account back — safely and quickly.

If your Facebook or other account was hacked or disabled, it can often be recovered

Losing access to your social account is alarming — but in most cases accounts that were hacked, taken over, or disabled can be restored if the right steps are taken quickly. This page explains what to do, what to avoid, and how to get professional help to recover your account.

Recommended steps (do these first):
  1. Secure your email & phone: Make sure the email and phone number connected to the account are secure and still accessible to you.
  2. Use official recovery pages: Follow the platform’s account recovery or appeal flow (e.g., Facebook Help Center). Never share your password in public.
  3. Collect proof: Prepare evidence such as account creation details, recent login locations, screenshots of suspicious activity, or copies of ID if required by the platform.
  4. Change passwords & enable 2FA: On any linked accounts (email, other social), change passwords and enable two-factor authentication immediately.
  5. Report impersonation & fraud: If scammers are using your account to defraud others, report it to the platform and to any affected contacts.
